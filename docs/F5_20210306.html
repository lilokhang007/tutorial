Chemical Equilibrium

Key Points:

In an equilibrium,

  • forward reaction rate = backward reaction rate.
  • both reaction rates are not zero.
  • no reactants are limiting. No chemicals reach zero concentration.

For a reversible reaction aA + bB ⇌ cC + dD

  • equilibrium constant Kc = [A]a[B]b/[C]c[D]d.
  • equilibrium constant Kc is a constant. Only increased when reaction temperature increases.

Questions:

  1. Sulfur trioxide decomposes into oxygen and sulphur dioxide. The reaction is reversible.
    1. Write down the balanced chemical equation.
    2. When the system is compressed, explain what happens to the (i) equilibrium position, and (ii) rate of decomposition of sulphur trioxide, and (iii) rate of formation of sulphur trioxide.

  2. A mixture of H2 and I2 was maintained at 740 K until the system reached equilibrium. The equilibrium mixture contained 1.37×10−2 M HI, 6.47×10−3 M H2, and 5.94×10−4 M I2.

    Calculate the reaction constant at this temperature.


  3. A 1.00 mol sample of NOCl was placed in a 2.00 L reactor and heated to 227°C until the system reached equilibrium. The contents of the reactor were then analyzed and found to contain 0.056 mol of Cl2.

    2NOCl(g) ⇌ 2NO(g) + Cl2(g)

    1. Calculate the reaction constant at this temperature.
    2. Mary says that when the system is compressed, the rate of decomposition of NOCl decreases. Do you agree with Mary? Comment on her statement.

  4. The water–gas shift reaction is important in several chemical processes, such as the production of H2 for fuel cells. This reaction can be written as follows:

    H2(g) + CO2(g) ⇌ H2O(g) + CO(g)

    Kc = 0.106 at 700 K. A mixture of gases that initially contains 0.0150 M H2 and 0.0150 M CO2 is allowed to equilibrate at 700 K.
    1. What are the final concentrations of all substances present?
    2. It is given that the volume of the system is 2.00 dm3. 0.02 mol of H2 is bumped into the system. Calculate the resultant concentrations of all substances.

  5. Given:

    CH3COOH(aq) ⇌ CH3COO-(aq) + H+(aq) Kc0 = 1.7×10-5

    H2SO4(aq) ⇌ HSO4-(aq) + H+(aq) Kc1 = 1×102

    HSO4-(aq) ⇌ SO42-(aq) + H+(aq) Kc2 = 1×10-2

    1. 0.05M HCl (aq)

    3. a mixture of equal volume 0.10M HCl (aq) and 0.05M NaOH (aq)
    4. 0.05M CH3COOH (aq).
    5. 0.05M H2SO4 (aq).
    6. 5g of NaHSO4 (s) dissolved in 0.1dm3 of water.
    7. 5g of Na2SO4 (s) dissolved in 0.1dm3 of water.
    8. a mixture of 0.5dm3 of 0.05M CH3COOH (aq) and 0.05dm3 of 1M HCl(aq).
