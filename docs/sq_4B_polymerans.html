Polymerization

Question 1

Low-density polyethene (LDPE) and Nylon 6-6 are two different kinds of plastics.

Explain their differences in terms of synthetic routes and physical properties.

LDPE forms by addition polymerization from ethene.

Nylon 6-6 forms by condensation polymerization from hexane-1,6-diamine and hexane-1,6-dioic acid.

Polymers have a moderately high melting point because of their high molecular mass.

LDPE has a lower melting point (~110°C) because of weak van der Waal's force between LDPE molecules.

Nylon 6-6 has a high melting point (~270°C) because of a crystalline structure by strong hydrogen bonds.
