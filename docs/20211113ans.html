Question 2

Outline a synthetic route to convert the following:

a) oct-3-ene -> octane

Pt/H2

b) butan-1-ol -> 1-chlorobutane

HCl

c) 1-bromobutane -> 2-bromobutane

NaOH; conc. H2SO4; HBr

d) butan-1-ol -> butanone

conc. H2SO4; HBr; NaOH; Cr2O72-/H+

e) hexanol -> hexyl hexanoate

hexanoic acid: Cr2O72-/H+

(esterification): hexanol + hexanoic acid (dilute H2SO4)

f) methyl ethanoate -> ethyl methanoate

(acid hydrolysis): -> methanol + ethanoic acid

methanol -> methanoic acid (Cr2O72-)

ethanoic acid -> ethanol (1. LiAlH4, dry ether; 2. H+)

(esterification): methanol + ethanoic acid (dilute H2SO4)

Question 4

Explain the difference of boiling points for sodium ethanoate, ethanoic acid, ethane.

Sodium ethanoate: giant ionic structure, strong ionic bonds

Ethanoic acid: simple molecular structure, van der Waal's forces, hydrogen bonds

Ethane: simple molecular structure, van der Waal's forces
